About

Background

I am an applied mathematician who has completed a Bachelors and a Masters in Applied Mathematics at the University of Auckland. My Masters thesis developed methods to approximate the risk of tipping in tipping-point systems. That work sits at the intersection of stochastic analysis and real-world systems modelling. Consequently I have recently become drawn towards modelling the effects of variable renewable energy in electricity networks/markets. I'm particularly interested in applying my stochastic modelling knowledge to tackle the challenges (and opportunities) that the uncertainty and variability of renewables presents.

Recent work

2025–2026

Masters Student

Working under the supervision of Professor Graham Donovan, I completed my Masters of Science in Applied Mathematics with a thesis focused on tipping-point systems. Specifically the Fokker-Planck equation was studied, for which I developed eigenmode approximations to estimate tipping risk.

2023-2025

Data Scientist

I spent approximately 18 months (split between full-time and part-time work) developing machine learning models for Shift, an Australian financial company. The models were used to assess credit risk and inform lending decisions.

2021–2024

Undergraduate Student

I completed my Bachelor of Science (Hons) in Applied Mathematics at the University of Auckland. During this time I developed a strong foundation in applied mathematics, including courses in differential equations, linear algebra, numerical methods and machine learning.
