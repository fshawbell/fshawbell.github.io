Felix Shaw-Bell

About

I am an applied mathematician who has completed my Bachelors and Masters in Applied Mathematics at the University of Auckland. My Masters thesis developed methods to approximate the risk of tipping in tipping-point systems.

That work sits at the intersection of stochastic analysis and real-world modelling. Consequently I have recently become drawn towards modelling the effects of variable renewable energy in electricity networks/markets. I'm particularly interested in applying my stochastic modelling knowledge to tackle the challenges (and opportunities) that the uncertainty and variability of renewables present.

Outside of mathematics, I enjoy playing sports and exploring the outdoors. I had the privilege of growing up in an adventurous family, with tramping and camping trips around New Zealand being our preferred family holiday. See my Trail Map for a compilation of some of my favorite tramping experiences.

I have also spent time working in the wine industry, and have a strong interest in wine and food. I have worked on vineyards in Marlborough and Waiheke Island, and have also worked in fine dining in Auckland. See my Cellar List for more information!

For a full list of my qualifications and experience, please view my CV (PDF), otherwise see a breif summary below.

Cellar List

Alongside my mathematical pursuits, I also have an interest in wine (and very nearly chose this as my career path) and food. I spent one summer working on various vineyards in the Marlborough Wine Region, and another summer working for Stonyridge Vineyard on Waiheke Island. After returning to Auckland I worked at Sidart Restaurant (now closed), initially as a commis waiter and later as a senior waiter and restaurant supervisor. While at Sidart I was exposed to the world of fine dining and wine pairing and had the privilege of working with some exceptional chefs and sommeliers. During this time I was able to earn my WSET Level 2 Award in Wines from the NZ School of Food and Wine and included wine science in my BSc coursework at the University of Auckland. See below a list of some of my favourite wines that I have had the pleasure of tasting and enjoying.

Babich Sauvignon Blanc

2020
Marlborough, NZ Sauvignon Blanc Tasted Late 2020

A classic Sauvignon Blanc in many regards, but one that will always stick with me. While working in the Marlborough wine region the contracting company I was working for spent a few weeks at the Babich vineyard. Later that year when the 2020 vintage was released I purchased a few bottles and had the pleasure of tasting it with my friends who had also work on the vineyard with me.

Nautilus Albariño

2020
Marlborough, NZ Albariño Tasted Mid 2021

Well after I become interested in viticulture and the history of wine, I was still unable to deduce anthing of significance about a given wine during a tasting. The first time that I tasted a wine and I could actually pick out the individual flavours and aromas was when I tasted this wine. That bright pineapple note sticks with me to this day.

Stonyridge 'Airfield'

2019
Waiheke Island, NZ Bordeaux-style Blend Tasted Mid 2021

'Airfield' is the second label of Stonyridge Vineyard, and is a Bordeaux-style blend. I was fortunate enough to be gifted a bottle as a leaving present when I finished my time working at Stonyridge. I remember opening it with my family while on a winter holiday in Raglan, and it was a very memorable experience. We were enjoying a hearty lamb roast, to which the wine paired beautifully.

Bollinger 'La Grande Année'

2012
Champagne, FR Pinot Noir, Chardonnay Tasted Early 2023

While working at Sidart Restaurant I had the privilege of tasting several exceptional wines, but this is the one that sticks with me the most. I first tasted it hastely in the middle of a busy service, but later that evening I was able to enjoy a full glass of it with my colleagues. To this day there is no other wine that I have tasted that has had the same level of complexity and depth of flavour.
