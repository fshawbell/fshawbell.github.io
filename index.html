Felix Shaw-Bell

About

I am an applied mathematician who has completed my Bachelors and Masters in Applied Mathematics at the University of Auckland. My Masters thesis developed methods to approximate the risk of tipping in tipping-point systems. That work sits at the intersection of stochastic analysis and real-world systems modelling. Consequently I have recently become drawn towards modelling the effects of variable renewable energy in electricity networks/markets. I'm particularly interested in applying my stochastic modelling knowledge to tackle the challenges (and opportunities) that the uncertainty and variability of renewables presents.
